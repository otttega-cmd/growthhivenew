Eyowo · A Growthhive Campaign Concept

Eyowo the brand

The cowrie was the first money West Africa ever knew. Ẹyọ — a single unit, a single cowrie — is inside the Yoruba word for money. And it is inside the brand's own name. The campaign does not invent a connection. It surfaces one that was always there.

01 — The Teaser

One question.
No brand.
The whole country asks.

Phase 1 goes up with no Eyowo logo. No URL. No tagline. Just a field of cowries and a question in Yoruba — on billboards, bus shelters, and walls across Lagos. The city starts talking before the brand has said a word.

Cowrie field · Question only · No attribution

Kí l'ẹyọ?
02 — ẹyọ

Ẹyọ ni ìbẹ̀rẹ̀.

Money is a beginning.

a beginning

One cowrie. The smallest denomination. Your phone number. The first account. The entry point to everything Eyowo offers — and the thing the brand owes every Nigerian it ever served.

03 — ẹyọ meji

Ẹyọ ni ìgbẹ́kẹ̀lé.

Money is trust.

trust

Two cowries. You and another person. Every transaction is an act of faith between two parties — the oldest truth in money. The one Eyowo broke, and the one it is now rebuilding.

04 — ẹyọ márùn

Ẹyọ ni ìdàgbàsókè.

Money is growth.

growth

A handful. A meaningful amount. Savings growing at 18%. Dreams accumulating — the house, the car, the school fees. Five cowries is the moment money starts becoming something.

05 — ẹyọ mẹwaa

Ẹyọ l'ẹyan.

Money is people.

people

Ten cowries. A community counted out. Money has no meaning without the people it moves between — the sender and the receiver, the saver and the dreamer. This is what the cowrie always knew. This is what Eyowo is returning to.

06 — The source

The cowrie was never
just currency.

In Yoruba life, the cowrie (owó ẹyọ) is worn, counted, cast, and consecrated. It appears in divination, in attire, in the names of children. It is older than any bank. It is the symbol of wealth that belongs to people — not institutions. Kí l'ẹyọ? borrows from a tradition the whole country already knows in its bones.

Note: Yoruba copy reviewed for cultural accuracy. Native speaker sign-off recommended before final production.

07 — The answer

Ẹyọ l'ẹyan.

08 — The system

A question that goes up
around the country.

Three phases. The question asks. The count answers. The brand arrives — not with noise, but with a name that was inside the answer all along.

01
The Question

Posters and billboards go up across Lagos and beyond with one thing on them: a field of cowries and the question "Kí l'ẹyọ?" No brand name. No URL. No logo. The country talks.

OOH · Lagos Island · Mainland

02
The Count

The counting series goes up — ẹyọ, ẹyọ meji, ẹyọ márùn, ẹyọ mẹwaa — each with its answer line. The posters tell a story in sequence. Social follows the same cadence, one post per week.

Sequential poster reveal

03
The Return

"Ẹyọ l'ẹyan." — the final answer — goes up. Eyowo's name appears for the first time in the campaign. The connection between ẹyọ, owó, and Eyowo is made visible. The brand re-enters culture.
