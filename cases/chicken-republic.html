01 — The Problem

The most eaten, least loved brand in Nigerian fast food.

Chicken Republic has won the market without winning the culture. Twenty years in. Over 300 locations. ₦60.9 billion in annual sales — outselling its nearest rival 13×.

And yet a brand that millions eat monthly has just 91,000 Instagram followers. Those three numbers should not coexist. They coexist because the brand has a transaction relationship with its customers — not an identity one.

Lagosians eat Chicken Republic the way they breathe Lagos air — without thinking, without identity, without loyalty that goes deeper than convenience. The brand is consumed without being claimed.

Nobody posts their Chicken Republic meal the way they post their KFC bucket. There's no social currency in it — because the brand's marketing has spent twenty years speaking to the transaction, and never once to the relationship.

The Insight

Every Nigerian who has ever walked into a Chicken Republic has a default order. Not something they think about — something they say automatically. As personal as a signature. As automatic as breathing.

The campaign asks for almost nothing. The output is everything.

05 — Campaign Architecture

Five phases. One question.

01
Weeks 1–4 · In-store · OOH · Social
The Ask
Every location gets a complete visual takeover — tray liners, table tents, cups, delivery bags — carrying one question and a QR code. Nothing else. Social goes into a 72-hour blackout. The silence signals something is different.
02
Weeks 3–8 · Live Digital OOH · Press
The Boards
Orders stream in real time to live digital billboards across Lagos, Abuja, Port Harcourt and Kano. Lagos sees itself scroll past on the Third Mainland Bridge. A full-page newspaper spread prints the first 14 days of orders like a national census: "This is Nigeria's Order."
03
Weeks 5–10 · Digital + In-store Print
The Hunger Map
A street-level map of every activated city, every outlet marked. Tap an outlet and it explodes into the orders submitted from that exact location, in the submitter's own words. Data journalism native to Lagos culture — and a press story every food writer covers.
04
Weeks 2–12 · Chowdeck · Glovo · Jumia
Delivery Rewards
Every delivery goes out in a campaign bag with its own unique QR code. Roughly 1 in 50 wins a complimentary "Still Yours" menu item. Walk-ins who submit enter a daily draw — the grand prize being your name and order on a Lagos billboard for 24 hours. The prize is visibility itself.
05
Month 3 · All Channels
Nigeria's Order
The most-submitted order across the campaign — chosen by Nigeria, not by marketing — is declared and given limited-edition packaging. The campaign culminates in a new permanent menu item built from the data: the first thing Nigeria chose for itself.
The Payoff

This brand belongs to Nigeria.
And Nigeria has been claiming it
one order at a time.

"Still Yours" is not a tagline. It's the beginning of a brand platform Chicken Republic can operate within for the next ten years — twenty years of Nigerian loyalty, finally named.
