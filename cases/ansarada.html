01 — The Brief

Ansarada.
In Africa.
Through the advisors.

Client Ansarada (a Datasite company) Product Ansarada Procure — purpose-built software for large-scale infrastructure procurement and tenders Market Pan-African · Lagos, Nairobi, Cairo, Cape Town Growthhive's Role Event concept · Brand identity · Invitation strategy · Production · Follow-up Format This is an active Growthhive pitch. Projected outcomes are clearly labelled.

Ansarada Procure is the only software solution purpose-built for large, complex, high-value infrastructure procurement. It brings structured workflow, secure document management, two-way Q&A, bidder management, and evaluation tools to a process that, in most African markets, has never had purpose-built technology applied to it.

Africa's infrastructure investment requirement — estimated at $130–170 billion annually — is being managed on spreadsheets, disconnected email threads, and institutional goodwill. Projects stall. Tenders are disputed. Budgets overrun. Advisors are blamed.

Ansarada's path to that market runs through the advisory community. The infrastructure advisory firms — Big 4 teams, specialist procurement advisors, infrastructure law firms, investment banks — are already trusted by the government clients who commission the projects. When an advisor recommends a tool, it is adopted. Win them, and Ansarada Procure becomes their standard recommendation to every government client they serve.

Growthhive proposes a pan-African roadshow that puts Ansarada in the same room as Africa's infrastructure advisory community across four cities. The event creates the conditions for demonstration, advocacy, and sale — without feeling like a sales event.

02 — The Insight

The advisor is
the distribution
channel.

Ansarada's direct sales motion to government infrastructure bodies is a long, relationship-dependent cycle. It requires credibility that takes years to build with procurement officers who move slowly and buy on trust.

The infrastructure advisor already has that credibility. They are already in the room. Their recommendation carries the weight of a professional mandate. The event is not the sale — the advisor who attends is.

Big 4
PwC, Deloitte, KPMG, EY infrastructure advisory teams — operating across all four Tender cities
Specialist
Steer Group, AECOM, WSP, Mott MacDonald — specialist procurement and infrastructure advisory firms
Legal
Infrastructure and government procurement law firms with active tender management mandates
Financial
Investment banks and DFI advisors with infrastructure project mandates across Africa
The B2B2G model
Growthhive reaches the advisor. The advisor recommends Procure. The government buys.
Business → Business → Government. The event is the top of the funnel. The advisor relationship is the channel. The government contract is the outcome.
Why this works
The advisor-referral motion is already how procurement software spreads — a trusted advisor recommends a tool, and it gets adopted across every client they serve. Tender systematises that dynamic deliberately rather than waiting for it to happen organically.
03 — The Concept
Tender.
How Africa procures.

The word advisors use every day, for the work they do every day. To tender, to issue a tender, to manage a tender — it is the vocabulary of their professional lives. An event named after that word is immediately, unmistakably theirs.

Tender is not positioned as an Ansarada product launch. It is positioned as a forum for Africa's infrastructure advisory community — one that advisors want to attend on their own terms, for the peer access, the content, and the professional standing. The commercial architecture sits beneath a format that has genuine value.

The product demonstration is woven into the content. The working session is not a sales pitch bolted on at the end. Advisors experience Ansarada Procure as part of a live procurement workflow exercise — they leave having used it, not just heard about it.

Peer access
The only forum for Africa's infrastructure advisory community. The people in the room are the people who matter.
Real content
The state of African infrastructure procurement, real project case studies, what's changing and what isn't.
Standing
Being at Tender signals currency. Not being there is visible. The event becomes a professional marker.
Practical value
They leave with tools, frameworks, and a product they've used — ready to recommend to their next government client.

05 — The Format

Half-day forum.
The whole morning.

Each city stop runs as a working morning — four sessions, each building on the last — followed by a networking lunch where Ansarada's team is available for individual conversations. The lunch is the commercial room.

08:30
Registration & Networking Breakfast
Advisors arrive and connect before the programme begins. Name-matched seating to engineer useful introductions.
09:30
Opening Keynote — Africa's Infrastructure Decade: Why the Process Determines the Outcome
The scale of Africa's infrastructure programme, where procurement is failing projects, and what best practice looks like globally. Sets the intellectual context for everything that follows.
10:30
Panel — The Advisor's Role in Procurement Excellence
Senior advisors from across the region discuss real projects — what went wrong, what made the difference, what they wish the process had looked like. Peer-to-peer, honest, structured.
11:45
Working Session — Running a Procurement with Ansarada Procure
Not a product demo. A live procurement workflow session — advisors work through a real-world infrastructure tender scenario using Ansarada Procure. The tool is experienced, not presented. The objective: attendees leave having used it, not just having heard about it.
13:00
Networking Lunch
The commercial room. Ansarada's team available for individual conversations. Post-event demonstration scheduling happens here. All attendees captured in CRM for follow-up sequencing.
