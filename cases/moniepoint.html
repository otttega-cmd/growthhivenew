Moniepoint × Growthhive · Product Marketing Concept

Moniepoint is
the middle point.

Every naira that moves through a Nigerian business passes through somewhere. A product marketing campaign built on the role Moniepoint plays — not the features it lists.

Speculative concept · A Growthhive demonstration · Not a commissioned engagement
Follow the money
01 — The insight

Nobody loves a POS terminal.
Everybody depends on
what it does.

Moniepoint's marketing problem isn't awareness — the blue terminal is on every counter in Lagos. The problem is meaning. Fintech advertising in Nigeria is a wall of identical claims: fast settlement, low fees, reliable uptime. Features. Specs. Noise. When everyone says the same thing, nobody is saying anything.

So we went looking for the thing only Moniepoint can claim, and found it in plain sight: scale itself. Most of the everyday transactions in Nigeria already pass through Moniepoint. The student's airtime, the trader's change, the okada fare, the shop's daily sales — somewhere in the middle of all of it sits the same infrastructure.

That's not a feature. That's a role. And a role is something you can own.

✕ The feature claim
"99.9% uptime. Instant settlement. Low fees." True of Moniepoint — and claimed by every competitor on the same street.
→
✓ The role claim
"The point every transaction passes through." Only the market leader can say it. Saying it makes the leadership visible.
02 — The positioning

For the Nigerian business owner who can't afford a single failed transaction, Moniepoint is the middle point — the reliable infrastructure every naira passes through, so the business never stops moving.

03 — Who it's for

Not "SMEs."
The person behind
the counter.

The strategy is built on one persona, drawn from how the product is actually used: the everyday merchant. Their relationship with payments isn't aspirational — it's existential. A failed transaction with a customer standing at the counter isn't an inconvenience. It's a lost sale, a lost customer, and a small humiliation. Every message in the campaign is written to that fear and its opposite: the quiet confidence of infrastructure that simply works.

Primary persona — The Everyday Merchant
Who Shop owner, market trader, SME operator — the person at the counter
Job Take payment reliably, every time, without losing a sale to downtime
Fear The network failing mid-transaction with a customer standing there
Outcome A business that never has to say "the POS is not working"
04 — The campaign

Follow the
₦5,000.

Five ₦1,000 notes leave a student's pocket on a Lagos morning. By nightfall they've passed through five pairs of hands — and every hand they passed through, Moniepoint was the middle point. A five-poster chain campaign where each poster hands the money to the next.

05 — The system behind it

A campaign on top.
A funnel underneath.

"Follow the ₦5,000" is the visible layer of a complete product marketing system — positioning, go-to-market playbook, full creative production, and Meta and Google campaigns engineered around the merchant funnel.

OOH chain placements sequenced along real Lagos commuter routes — each poster physically located where its scene happens, so the campaign maps onto the city itself.

Meta and Google performance layers retarget by funnel stage: awareness film for cold audiences, merchant testimony for considerers, and an activation offer at the decision point — every asset speaking the role, not the spec sheet.
