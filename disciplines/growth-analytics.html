01 — The belief
What we think about analytics.

Most agencies report
what happened. We measure
to change what's next.

A report that tells you what already happened is a history lesson. Useful, occasionally interesting, but it doesn't move anything. The only analytics that matters is analytics that changes a decision — that tells you to shift budget from this channel to that one, to fix the step in the funnel where everyone drops out, to double down on the cohort that actually sticks around.

This is where being a full agency matters. We don't hand you a dashboard and leave you to interpret it. We run the campaigns the data is measuring — so the insight feeds straight back into the next action, with no gap between knowing and doing. The loop closes inside one team.

And the rigour is real. Clean tracking infrastructure, honest attribution that doesn't flatter the easy channels, experiments designed to actually prove causation. Beautiful charts built on bad data are worse than no charts at all — they give you confidence in the wrong direction.

✕ Reporting
"Last month, ROAS was 3.2× and CTR rose 0.4%." True. Tidy. Changes nothing about what you do tomorrow.
✓ Decision analytics
"TikTok converts at half the cost of Meta — move 30% of the Meta budget there next week." Same data. Now it's an instruction.
02 — What's included

Nine ways
to read the truth.

Growth analytics at Growthhive spans the full stack — from the tracking infrastructure that produces clean data, through the analysis that finds the signal, to the experiments that prove what actually causes growth. Each capability stands alone. Together they form a complete measurement system.

01
Attribution Modelling & Measurement Frameworks
Multi-touch and channel attribution, plus the measurement framework that defines what success means and how it's tracked. Honest attribution that credits the channels actually doing the work — not just the last click.
02
Funnel Analysis & Drop-off Identification
Mapping every stage from awareness to conversion and finding precisely where users fall out — and why. The fastest path to recovered revenue is usually fixing the leak you didn't know was there.
03
Cohort Analysis & Retention
Tracking groups of users over time to understand retention, lifetime value, and how behaviour differs by acquisition source or period. Acquisition is vanity; retention is where the business actually lives.
04
A/B Testing & Experimentation
Designing, running, and reading experiments that prove causation — not correlation. Properly powered tests, clean control groups, and honest readouts that tell you what genuinely moved the metric.
05
Conversion Analytics & CRO
Measuring and improving the rate at which traffic becomes customers — the analytics layer beneath conversion rate optimisation. Where the experience, the messaging, and the data meet.
06
Performance Reporting & Dashboards
Live dashboards and reports that make the data legible and, more importantly, actionable. Built around the decisions they need to support — not around every metric it's possible to display.
07
Data Infrastructure & Tracking Setup
The foundation everything depends on — pixels, event tracking, tag management, and a clean data pipeline. Get this wrong and every insight downstream is built on sand. We get it right first.
08
ROI & Spend Efficiency Analysis
Connecting spend directly to outcome — which naira, pound, and dollar is working, which is wasted, and exactly where to reallocate. The analysis that pays for itself by cutting the spend that wasn't.
09
Marketing & Campaign Analytics
The cross-channel performance layer — how campaigns perform across every platform and market, read as one coherent picture instead of seven disconnected dashboards. The view that lets you actually compare.
03 — The method

Analytics that
closes the loop.

Measurement only creates value when it changes the next action. Because Growthhive runs the campaigns we measure, the loop closes inside one team — no handoff, no gap between knowing and doing. This is the cycle every engagement runs on.

01

04 — Reading the data

A number is just
a number until you see
the pattern.

A retention cohort table is a good example of why visualisation matters. The raw numbers tell you retention rates. The heatmap tells you something the table hides — that a specific acquisition period produced users who behaved completely differently, and that the drop-off concentrates in a particular week.

This is what we mean by reading the truth. The data has a shape. Our job is to find it, show it clearly, and turn it into the decision it implies — which channel to scale, which week to intervene, which cohort to study.

05 — How it works

Five stages.
Data to decision.

The engagement follows the same Growthhive structure, built for analytics — it starts by getting the measurement right and ends in a system that keeps producing decisions, not just reports.

01
Discovery Call
30 minutes, free, no preparation. We want to understand the business goals, the channels in play, what's currently being tracked, and the decisions you're trying to make better. What you need to know that you can't see right now.
02
Tracking & Data Audit
We audit the current measurement setup — what's tracked, what's broken, what's missing, and whether the data can be trusted. Bad tracking produces confident wrong answers, so we fix the foundation before we analyse anything. This becomes the brief.
03
Framework & Scope Review
We share the audit and propose the measurement framework — the metrics that matter, the attribution model, the dashboards, and the analysis cadence. We agree the scope: a one-time audit and setup, or an ongoing analytics engagement. Confirmed before any build.
04
Three Measurement Approaches
We present three distinct ways to structure the measurement — different attribution models, different metric hierarchies, different dashboard philosophies. Each implies a different view of what drives the business. You choose the one that fits how decisions actually get made.
05
Build, Measure & Iterate
We build the tracking, the dashboards, and the analysis system — then run the loop. Regular readouts that end in recommendations, experiments that prove what works, and continuous refinement. For ongoing engagements, every cycle feeds the next decision.
